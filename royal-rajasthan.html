Package Overview

A Kingdom of Colour & Sand

Rajasthan is India's largest state and its most visually dramatic. This Royal Rajasthan circuit connects four of its greatest cities in a single, unforgettable road journey — each with its own colour, character and centuries of royal history.

Begin in Jaipur — the Pink City — with its hilltop forts, painted palaces, and bazaars overflowing with gems, fabrics, and spices. Drive west to Jodhpur — the Blue City — where the sky-blue painted old town clusters beneath the towering walls of Mehrangarh Fort. Continue deep into the Thar Desert to reach Jaisalmer, a golden sandstone city rising from the dunes, where you can spend a night under stars at a desert camp with camel rides and folk music.

The circuit ends in Udaipur — the City of Lakes — arguably India's most romantic city, where white marble palaces float on shimmering lake waters and every rooftop restaurant faces a postcard sunset. Your private car and experienced local driver make navigating Rajasthan's long highways and narrow old-city lanes completely effortless.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

An immersive Rajasthan circuit spanning the Pink, Blue, Golden, and White cities of the Land of Kings.

01
Day 1–2
Jaipur — The Pink City
Arrive Jaipur. Day 1: Explore the imposing Amber Fort — elephant ride up the ramp, Sheesh Mahal (Hall of Mirrors), and panoramic views of Maota Lake. Afternoon at Jaigarh Fort (home of Asia's largest cannon on wheels). Day 2: Hawa Mahal (5 storeys of 953 windows), City Palace, Jantar Mantar astronomical observatory, and the old bazaar for block prints and blue pottery.
Amber Fort Hawa Mahal City Palace Jantar Mantar
03
Day 3
Jaipur to Jodhpur — The Blue City (via Pushkar)
Morning drive to Jodhpur (6 hrs) with an optional stop at the holy city of Pushkar — the sacred camel fair town with its Brahma temple and colourful ghats on a desert lake. Arrive Jodhpur by evening. Dinner with a view of the floodlit Mehrangarh Fort.
Pushkar Lake Brahma Temple Transfer to Jodhpur Mehrangarh Night View
04
Day 4
Jodhpur — Mehrangarh Fort & Blue City Lanes
Full day in Jodhpur. The massive Mehrangarh Fort is one of India's most spectacular — walk its seven gates, view the Zenana gallery, and gaze down at the blue-painted old city below. Afternoon walk through the narrow lanes of the old city — indigo-dyed walls, blue houses, the clock tower market, and Umaid Bhawan Palace (still a royal residence).
Mehrangarh Fort Blue City Walk Clock Tower Bazaar Umaid Bhawan Palace
05
Day 5
Jodhpur to Jaisalmer — The Golden City
Drive across the Thar Desert to Jaisalmer (approx. 5–6 hrs). The landscape transforms into a rolling golden desert — sparse villages, windmills, and camels by the roadside. Afternoon check-in. Sunset at Vyas Chhatri with panoramic desert views, or walk through the living fort — one of the last inhabited forts in the world.
Thar Desert Drive Jaisalmer Fort Vyas Chhatri Sunset Old City Walk
06
Day 6
Sam Sand Dunes — Desert Camp Night
Morning in Jaisalmer: Patwon ki Haveli (a cluster of five ornately carved sandstone mansions), Bada Bagh royal cenotaphs, and Gadsisar Lake. Afternoon drive to Sam Sand Dunes (45 mins). Camel ride at sunset, then an overnight in a luxury desert camp with folk music, Kalbelia dance, and a bonfire dinner under a sky full of stars.
Patwon ki Haveli Gadsisar Lake Camel Safari Desert Camp Stay
07
Day 7–8
Jaisalmer to Udaipur — City of Lakes (via Ranakpur)
Long but scenic drive to Udaipur (approx. 7–8 hrs) with a magnificent stop at the Ranakpur Jain Temples — one of the most elaborately carved temple complexes in India with 1,444 unique marble pillars. Arrive Udaipur in the evening. First view of Lake Pichola as the sun dips behind the Aravalli hills is unforgettable.
Ranakpur Jain Temple Aravalli Hills Drive Arrive Udaipur Lake Pichola View
09
Day 9–10
Udaipur — Palaces, Lakes & Sunset Terraces
Two days in Udaipur — arguably India's most romantic city. City Palace (a magnificent lakeside complex with museums, galleries, and terraced gardens), Lake Palace Hotel, Jagdish Temple, and the Saheliyon ki Bari gardens. Boat ride on Lake Pichola to Jag Mandir island. Sunset from the Monsoon Palace on the hilltop with a panorama of the city and lakes below.
City Palace Lake Pichola Boat Jag Mandir Island Monsoon Palace Sunset
11
Day 11–12 (Optional)
Departure / Kumbhalgarh Extension
Optional day trip to Kumbhalgarh Fort — whose walls stretch 36 km, the second longest wall in the world after the Great Wall of China. Or onward drive to Mumbai, departure from Udaipur Airport, or return to Jaipur/Delhi with an en-route stop at Chittorgarh Fort — the largest fort in India.
Kumbhalgarh Fort Chittorgarh Fort Udaipur Departure

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
October to February is ideal. March–April is still comfortable. Avoid May–June — Jaisalmer temperatures exceed 45°C. The Desert Festival in February is spectacular (check dates).
Desert Camp Tips
Book your Sam Dunes camp in advance during peak season (Nov–Feb). Carry warm layers — desert nights drop below 10°C. Avoid synthetic fabrics that don't breathe in daytime heat.
Photography
Golden hour in Jaisalmer and Jodhpur is extraordinary — warm light on sandstone and blue walls. The view of Mehrangarh from the clock tower is one of the best photos you'll take in India.
Shopping Tips
Jaipur for gems, block prints, and blue pottery. Jodhpur for antiques and handicrafts. Jaisalmer for camel leather goods and mirror-work textiles. Always bargain politely — expect to pay 40–60% of first quoted price.
