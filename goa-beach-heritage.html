Package Overview

Sun, Sand & Portuguese Soul

Goa is unlike anywhere else in India — a place where 450 years of Portuguese rule left behind whitewashed baroque churches, terracotta-roofed mansions, and a laid-back coastal culture that is entirely its own. The Goa Beach & Heritage Tour takes you through the full breadth of this tiny state — from its lively northern beaches to its quieter southern shores, with the charming capital Panaji at the heart of it all.

Explore Panaji's Latin Quarter (Fontainhas) with its candy-coloured houses and narrow European lanes. Drive to Old Goa for the magnificent Basilica of Bom Jesus — a UNESCO World Heritage Site housing the remains of St. Francis Xavier. Discover the lively beach shacks of Calangute and Baga in the north, then contrast it with the serene Palolem and Agonda beaches of the south. In between, a spice plantation tour and a sunset cruise on the Mandovi river complete this perfect coastal escape.

Your private driver navigates Goa's roads, ferries, and beach villages so you can drift between sun-drenched sands and centuries-old heritage with total ease.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

A perfectly balanced Goa route mixing beaches, history, and Goan culture. Flexible on request.

01
Day 1
Arrive Goa — Panaji & Latin Quarter
Your driver picks you up from Goa Airport (Dabolim or Mopa) or the railway station. Head to Panaji — Goa's relaxed capital city on the Mandovi river. Explore the Fontainhas Latin Quarter — a UNESCO heritage zone of cobblestone lanes, azulejo-tiled walls, and pastel Portuguese townhouses. Evening sunset cruise on the Mandovi river with Goan folk music on board.
Goa Arrival Fontainhas Quarter Church of Our Lady Mandovi Sunset Cruise
02
Day 2
Old Goa — UNESCO Churches & Heritage
Drive to Old Goa — once the most splendid city in Asia, now a remarkable open-air museum of Portuguese baroque architecture. Visit the Basilica of Bom Jesus (St. Francis Xavier's relics), the Sé Cathedral (largest church in Asia), and the Church of St. Francis of Assisi. Afternoon at the Goa State Museum and Institute Menezes Braganza for colonial-era artefacts.
Basilica of Bom Jesus Sé Cathedral Church of St. Francis Goa State Museum
03
Day 3
North Goa — Forts, Beaches & Beach Shacks
Drive north for a full day of Goa's iconic coastline. Begin at Aguada Fort — a 17th-century Portuguese lighthouse fort perched over the Arabian Sea. Continue to the buzzing beaches of Calangute and Baga for a leisurely lunch at a beach shack. Afternoon at Anjuna Beach and its famous Wednesday flea market (if visiting mid-week). Sunset at Vagator with views of Chapora Fort above.
Aguada Fort Calangute & Baga Anjuna Flea Market Vagator & Chapora
04
Day 4
Spice Plantation & Dudhsagar Falls
Morning visit to a traditional Goan spice plantation in the lush Western Ghats interior — a guided tour through nutmeg, cardamom, pepper, and vanilla vines followed by a traditional Goan lunch. Afternoon drive to the spectacular Dudhsagar Falls — one of India's tallest waterfalls, tumbling 310 m down a cliff face in four milky white tiers (seasonal — best Oct–May).
Spice Plantation Tour Goan Thali Lunch Dudhsagar Falls Western Ghats Drive
05
Day 5
South Goa — Serene Beaches & Quiet Coves
Drive south for a completely different Goa — quieter, greener, and more pristine. Stop at Colva Beach — South Goa's longest beach. Continue to the crescent-shaped Palolem Beach, one of India's most beautiful, framed by jungle headlands. Explore the untouched Agonda Beach — a nesting site for Olive Ridley sea turtles. Evening kayaking or paddleboarding in the calm Palolem bay.
Colva Beach Palolem Beach Agonda Beach Kayaking at Sunset
06
Day 6–7 (Optional Extension)
Leisure / Return
Optional extra day for a dolphin-watching boat trip at Palolem, a visit to Cabo de Rama Fort on a cliff above the sea, or a relaxed morning at Butterfly Beach (accessible by boat). Otherwise return to North Goa or Panaji for your departure flight from Goa Airport — just 15 minutes from the capital by car.
Dolphin Watching Cabo de Rama Fort Butterfly Beach Departure / Airport Drop

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
November to February is peak season — ideal weather, all beaches and activities fully open. October and March are quieter with good conditions. Avoid June–September (monsoon) — most beach shacks close and the sea is too rough for swimming.
Church Dress Code
Basilica of Bom Jesus and other heritage churches require covered shoulders and knees. Carry a light shawl. Beaches are relaxed — swimwear is fine on the beach but cover up before entering shops, restaurants, and markets.
Dudhsagar Falls
The falls are accessible by jeep safari from Colem village — the road is off-limits to private cars. We arrange jeep transfers for this excursion. Best visited October–January for full flow. Closed during heavy monsoon months (June–September).
Getting Around Goa
Goa's beaches are spread out — your private driver is the most comfortable way to hop between them without waiting for taxis. Some beaches (like Butterfly Beach) are only accessible by boat — our driver knows all the local boat operators to get you there.
