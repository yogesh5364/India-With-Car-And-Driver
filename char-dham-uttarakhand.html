Package Overview

India's Most Sacred Journey

The Char Dham Yatra is Hinduism's most revered pilgrimage circuit — four sacred shrines nestled deep in the Uttarakhand Himalayas, believed to wash away sins and grant moksha. This journey combines divine devotion with some of the most breathtaking mountain scenery on Earth.

Begin in Haridwar — where the holy Ganga descends from the mountains and the evening Ganga Aarti fills the ghats with fire and prayer. Move to Rishikesh, the yoga capital of the world, where ancient ashrams line the river and adventure awaits on its rushing rapids. Then climb into the Himalayas towards Kedarnath — Lord Shiva's highest abode — and complete the circuit at Badrinath, Lord Vishnu's sacred seat overlooking the Alaknanda river.

Your private driver navigates every mountain turn with skill and care — from the plains of Haridwar to the high-altitude passes near Badrinath. You focus on the journey; we handle the road.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

A carefully designed route that balances pilgrimage, sightseeing, and mountain driving. Flexible on request.

01
Day 1
Arrive Haridwar — Ganga Ghats & Holy Aarti
Arrive at Haridwar by road from Delhi (approx. 5 hrs). Check in and freshen up. Evening at Har Ki Pauri ghat for the legendary Ganga Aarti — fire, bells, and chanting as thousands of diyas float down the sacred river at dusk. One of India's most moving spectacles. Morning visit to Mansa Devi Temple by cable car.
Har Ki Pauri Ganga Aarti Mansa Devi Temple Haridwar Bazaar
02
Day 2
Rishikesh — Yoga Capital & Adventure Hub
Drive to Rishikesh (30 mins). Explore Laxman Jhula and Ram Jhula — iconic suspension bridges over the Ganga. Visit the iconic Beatles Ashram and riverside cafes. Optional white-water rafting on the Ganga (Grade 3–4 rapids). Evening attend the Parmarth Niketan Aarti — fire lamps reflecting off the river with the Himalayas as backdrop.
Laxman Jhula Beatles Ashram River Rafting (Optional) Parmarth Aarti
03
Day 3
Rishikesh to Guptkashi — Gateway to Kedarnath
Early morning drive through Devprayag (where the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers merge) and Rudraprayag, climbing steadily into the Garhwal Himalayas. Arrive Guptkashi by afternoon — a sacred town with views of the towering Kedarnath range. Evening visit to the ancient Vishwanath Temple in Guptkashi. Overnight rest before the Kedarnath trek.
Devprayag Sangam Rudraprayag Guptkashi Vishwanath Mountain Views
04
Day 4
Kedarnath — The Abode of Lord Shiva
Drive to Sonprayag and then Gaurikund (the trek base). Trek 18 km to Kedarnath temple (or take pony / helicopter if preferred) through breathtaking alpine meadows and snow-fed streams. Attend the evening aarti inside the ancient 8th-century stone temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Overnight at Kedarnath. Return trek to Gaurikund next morning.
Kedarnath Temple Evening Aarti Alpine Trek / Pony Overnight at Dham
05
Day 5
Kedarnath Return & Drive to Badrinath
Early morning darshan at Kedarnath before beginning the descent back to Gaurikund. Drive to Joshimath via the scenic Chopta meadows (detour optional) — known as the "Mini Switzerland of Uttarakhand". Arrive Joshimath / Badrinath valley by evening. En-route photo stops at Vishnu Prayag sangam.
Morning Darshan Descent Trek Chopta Meadows Joshimath Arrival
06
Day 6
Badrinath — Lord Vishnu's Himalayan Shrine
Drive from Joshimath to Badrinath (45 mins). Bathe in the hot Tapt Kund springs before the temple opens. Attend morning puja at the Badrinath temple — one of the Char Dhams and one of the 108 Divya Desams. Visit Mana Village — India's last village before the Tibet border — and the legendary Vyas Gufa. Return to Joshimath for the night.
Badrinath Temple Tapt Kund Bath Mana Village Vyas Gufa
07
Day 7–8 (Optional Extension / Return)
Valley of Flowers / Return to Haridwar & Delhi
Optional: trek to the Valley of Flowers National Park (UNESCO World Heritage Site) — a glacial valley carpeted with alpine wildflowers, accessible July–September. Or begin the return drive to Haridwar via Srinagar Garhwal (not Kashmir) and Devprayag. Overnight in Rishikesh, departure from Haridwar or Dehradun airport next morning.
Valley of Flowers (Optional) Srinagar Garhwal Return to Haridwar Departure Drop

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
May to June and September to October are ideal. The Char Dham temples open in April/May and close after Diwali. Avoid monsoon (July–August) due to landslide risks on mountain roads.
Clothing & Gear
Carry warm layers — temperatures near Kedarnath and Badrinath can drop below 5°C even in May. Good trekking shoes are essential. Rainwear is advisable throughout the season.
Altitude Precautions
Badrinath sits at 3,133m and Kedarnath at 3,583m. Acclimatize by spending at least 2 nights at medium altitude (Rishikesh/Guptkashi) before ascending. Carry altitude sickness medication as a precaution.
Yatra Registration
All Char Dham pilgrims must register on the official Uttarakhand government yatra portal. Biometric registration is required at base camps. We can assist with the process on request.
