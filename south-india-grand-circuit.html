Package Overview

India's Most Diverse Road Journey

The South India Grand Circuit is the subcontinent's most varied road adventure — threading through ancient Dravidian temple cities, French colonial boulevards, misty Nilgiri hill stations, wild coffee-scented forests, and the grand palace city of Mysore all in one sweeping loop.

Beginning in Chennai — South India's cultural and commercial capital — you travel down the Coromandel Coast to the pastel-painted streets of Pondicherry, once a French enclave. The route then turns inland to Madurai, where the towering gopurams of the Meenakshi Amman Temple dominate the skyline, before climbing into the cool Nilgiri Mountains to Ooty. From there the fragrant coffee and pepper estates of Coorg welcome you before the grand finale in Mysore — home to one of India's most spectacular palaces.

Your private driver navigates every coastal highway, ghat road, and city street with expertise. This is South India at its richest — one road, six destinations, infinite memories.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

A carefully designed route that balances sightseeing, travel time, and leisure. Flexible on request.

01
Day 1
Arrive Chennai — Marina Beach & Temple Heritage
Arrive at Chennai airport and meet your driver. Visit the Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Mylapore — one of Chennai's oldest Dravidian shrines. Stroll along Marina Beach, the world's second longest urban beach. Explore the Government Museum for a taste of South India's ancient bronze sculptures. Try a classic South Indian thali dinner at a local restaurant.
Kapaleeshwarar Temple Marina Beach Government Museum Mylapore Heritage Walk
02
Day 2
Mahabalipuram & Drive to Pondicherry
Morning drive south along the scenic East Coast Road to Mahabalipuram (1 hr). Explore the UNESCO World Heritage rock-cut temples — Shore Temple, Arjuna's Penance, and the Five Rathas hewn from single boulders. Continue south to Pondicherry (1.5 hrs). Evening walk through the French Quarter — pastel villas, baguette bakeries, and a coastal promenade.
Shore Temple Arjuna's Penance Five Rathas French Quarter Pondi
03
Day 3
Pondicherry — Auroville & Coastal Calm
Morning visit to Auroville — the international spiritual township and Matrimandir. Explore the Tamil Quarter's vibrant temples and the colonial Sri Aurobindo Ashram. Afternoon cycling through White Town's pastel streets or a quiet morning meditation session. Sunset by the Pondicherry Promenade with crashing waves and golden light.
Auroville Sri Aurobindo Ashram White Town Cycling Promenade Sunset
04
Day 4
Drive to Thanjavur & Onward to Madurai
Drive west to Thanjavur (Tanjore) — home to the magnificent Brihadeeswarar Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a masterpiece of Chola architecture. The 1,000-year-old vimana tower casts no shadow at noon. Visit the Thanjavur Palace and its art gallery of exquisite Chola bronzes. Continue to Madurai in the evening (2.5 hrs).
Brihadeeswarar Temple Thanjavur Palace Chola Bronze Gallery Transfer to Madurai
05
Day 5
Madurai — The Temple City of South India
Full day in Madurai. The Meenakshi Amman Temple is one of India's greatest architectural achievements — its 14 gopurams covered in thousands of painted deities soar over the city. Attend the evening deity procession inside the temple (around 9 PM). Visit the Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace and explore the famous Gandhi Museum. Taste Madurai's iconic filter coffee and jigarthanda.
Meenakshi Amman Temple Evening Procession Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace Gandhi Museum
06
Day 6
Climb to Ooty — The Queen of Hill Stations
Drive north from Madurai to Ooty (approx. 4.5 hrs) through the Palani Hills and into the Nilgiris. Option to ride the famous Nilgiri Mountain Railway (UNESCO World Heritage) from Mettupalayam to Ooty — a century-old steam train winding through tea gardens and tunnels. Arrive Ooty, stroll through the Botanical Gardens and Rose Garden. Enjoy the crisp mountain air at 2,240m altitude.
Nilgiri Mountain Railway Botanical Garden Rose Garden Ooty Lake
07
Day 7
Ooty to Coorg — Coffee Country
Drive northwest through the Nilgiris and Mysore plateau to Coorg (Kodagu) — approx. 3.5 hrs. Visit a working coffee and pepper plantation and taste freshly brewed estate coffee. Explore the Abbey Falls and Raja's Seat — a hilltop garden with sweeping Coorg valley views. Coorg is India's largest coffee producer and has a culture and cuisine entirely its own.
Coffee Estate Tour Abbey Falls Raja's Seat Coorg Cuisine
08
Day 8
Coorg to Mysore — Palace City of Karnataka
Drive to Mysore (approx. 2 hrs). The Mysore Palace — one of India's most visited monuments — is a fantasy of Indo-Saracenic architecture, at its most magical when 97,000 bulbs illuminate it on Sunday evenings. Visit the Chamundeshwari Temple on Chamundi Hill and the lively Devaraja Market. Optional day trip to the Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary.
Mysore Palace Chamundi Hill Temple Devaraja Market Bird Sanctuary (Optional)
09
Day 9–10 (Optional Extension / Departure)
Mysore — Bangalore or Onwards
Optional extension to Belur and Halebidu — the jewelled Hoysala temples featuring some of the most intricate stone carving in all of India. Alternatively drive to Bangalore (3 hrs) for international departure. Or explore Srirangapatna — Tipu Sultan's island fortress — and the serene Brindavan Gardens en route to Bangalore.
Belur & Halebidu (Optional) Srirangapatna Brindavan Gardens Bangalore Departure

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
October to February is ideal — cool and dry across South India. March–May is hot in the plains but pleasant in Ooty and Coorg. Avoid June–September (heavy monsoon in Kerala, Karnataka, and Coorg).
Temple Dress Code
Cover legs and shoulders at all Dravidian temples. Many large temples (Meenakshi, Brihadeeswarar) require foreigners to buy a camera ticket. Shoes must be removed at all temple entrances.
Mysore Palace Timings
Open daily 10 AM–5:30 PM. The palace illumination (97,000 bulbs) happens every Sunday evening and during Dasara festival — a truly spectacular sight. Arrive at least 30 minutes before lighting time.
Currency & Payments
Carry Indian Rupees (INR) for temple offerings, local markets, and rural areas. UPI and cards are accepted in cities and larger towns. Keep some cash handy for temple towns like Madurai.
