Package Overview

India's Most Iconic Circuit

The Golden Triangle is India's most celebrated road journey — connecting three of the country's most historically rich cities in a single, seamless loop. Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur together tell the story of Mughal empire, Rajput glory, and modern India all in one unforgettable trip.

Starting in the capital Delhi with its Mughal monuments, colonial boulevards, and vibrant street food, you drive south to Agra — home to the timeless Taj Mahal and the mighty Agra Fort. From there the Pink City of Jaipur completes the triangle with its Amber Fort perched on golden hills, the fairytale Hawa Mahal, and the bustling bazaars of the old city.

All three cities are connected by well-maintained national highways, making this the perfect introduction to India by road. Your private driver handles all navigation, parking, and logistics — all you carry is a sense of wonder.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

A carefully designed route that balances sightseeing, travel time, and leisure. Flexible on request.

01
Day 1
Arrive Delhi — Old City & Mughal Monuments
Your driver will pick you up from Delhi airport or your hotel. Begin with a guided drive through Old Delhi — Jama Masjid, Chandni Chowk, and a rickshaw ride through the spice market. Afternoon visit to the magnificent Red Fort (a UNESCO World Heritage Site). Evening at leisure in Connaught Place.
Red Fort Jama Masjid Chandni Chowk Spice Market
02
Day 2
New Delhi — Mughal & Colonial Heritage
Morning visit to Qutub Minar (India's tallest ancient minaret) and Humayun's Tomb — both UNESCO sites. Drive past India Gate, Rashtrapati Bhavan and Parliament House. Afternoon at Lotus Temple or the National Museum. Depart for Agra in the evening (approx. 3.5 hrs).
Qutub Minar Humayun's Tomb India Gate Transfer to Agra
03
Day 3
Agra — Taj Mahal at Sunrise & Agra Fort
Early morning at the Taj Mahal — dawn light on white marble is truly magical. Spend 2–3 hours at the monument before it fills with crowds. Post-breakfast visit to Agra Fort — a massive riverside fortress with palaces, mosques, and halls. Evening optional visit to Mehtab Bagh for a sunset silhouette view of the Taj.
Taj Mahal at Sunrise Agra Fort Mehtab Bagh Marble Craft Walk
04
Day 4
Fatehpur Sikri en route to Jaipur
Morning departure towards Jaipur with an en-route stop at Fatehpur Sikri — Akbar's ghost city of red sandstone palaces, courtyards and mosques, abandoned after just 15 years. Arrive Jaipur by afternoon. Evening free to stroll MI Road or the Pink City's lit-up bazaars.
Fatehpur Sikri Buland Darwaza Transfer to Jaipur Jaipur Bazaars
05
Day 5
Jaipur — Forts, Palaces & Pink City
Full day in Jaipur. Begin with Amber Fort — a hilltop Rajput fortress with painted halls and a breathtaking view. Continue to Jaigarh Fort overlooking Amber. Afternoon at Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds), City Palace, and the Jantar Mantar astronomical observatory. Sunset at Nahargarh Fort with panoramic city views.
Amber Fort Hawa Mahal City Palace Jantar Mantar Nahargarh Fort
06
Day 6–7 (Optional Extension)
Jaipur Leisure / Return to Delhi
Optional extra day in Jaipur for a block-printing workshop, Jaipur literature cafe, or a day trip to Abhaneri step well and Bhangarh Fort. Otherwise, morning drop-off at Jaipur Airport or drive back to Delhi (approx. 5 hrs) with en-route stop at Abhaneri or Neemrana Fort Palace.
Abhaneri Step Well Block Printing Workshop Neemrana Fort Departure / Delhi Drop

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
October to March is ideal — cool days, clear skies, perfect for outdoor monuments. Avoid May–June (extreme heat). Monsoon (July–Sept) is lush but humid.
Dress Code
Cover shoulders and knees at mosques and temples. Carry a light scarf. Comfortable walking shoes are essential — you'll walk a lot at the Taj Mahal and Agra Fort.
Taj Mahal Timings
Open sunrise to sunset, closed on Fridays. Pre-book tickets online to skip queues. Sunrise entry is magical — arrive 30 mins before opening gates.
Currency & Payments
Carry Indian Rupees (INR) for entry tickets and local shopping. ATMs widely available. UPI payments accepted almost everywhere in 2026.
