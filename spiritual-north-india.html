Package Overview

India's Most Sacred Circuit

The Spiritual North India tour traces the holiest corridor of the Indian subcontinent — a land where civilisations were born, rivers were worshipped, and gods walked among mortals. Varanasi, Sarnath, Allahabad (Prayagraj), and Ayodhya together form one of the most profound pilgrimage routes on earth, connecting Hindu, Buddhist, and ancient Indian heritage in a single journey.

Begin in Varanasi — the oldest living city in the world — where dawn boat rides on the Ganga and the evening Ganga Aarti ceremony on the ghats will stay with you forever. Drive to Sarnath, where the Buddha gave his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. Continue to Allahabad (Prayagraj) — the meeting point of three sacred rivers and the Sangam's spiritual power. End at Ayodhya, the birthplace of Lord Ram and home to the newly built Ram Mandir.

Your private driver handles all navigation through these ancient cities, allowing you to immerse yourself in each sacred site without the stress of logistics.

Day by Day

Your Itinerary

A spiritually enriching route that balances pilgrimage, sightseeing, and quiet reflection. Flexible on request.

01
Day 1
Arrive Varanasi — The Eternal City
Your driver picks you up from Varanasi airport or railway station. Check in and head to the ghats for your first glimpse of the Ganga. As evening falls, witness the magnificent Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat — an overwhelming spectacle of fire, chanting, and devotion that has taken place every single evening for centuries.
Varanasi Arrival Ghats Walk Dashashwamedh Ghat Ganga Aarti
02
Day 2
Varanasi — Sunrise Boat Ride & Kashi Vishwanath
Rise before dawn for a boat ride on the Ganga at sunrise — one of India's most iconic experiences. Watch the city come alive on the 84 ghats, from early morning bathers to sadhus in meditation. After breakfast, visit the newly renovated Kashi Vishwanath Temple Corridor — one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. Explore the ancient lanes of the old city and its silk-weaving workshops.
Sunrise Boat Ride Kashi Vishwanath Temple Old City Lanes Silk Weaving District
03
Day 3
Sarnath — Where the Buddha Taught
Drive 10 km to Sarnath — one of the four holiest sites in Buddhism. Visit the Dhamek Stupa marking the exact spot of the Buddha's first sermon. Explore the Sarnath Museum housing the famous Ashoka Lion Capital (India's national emblem). Walk through the monastery ruins and the serene Mulagandha Kuti Vihar. Return to Varanasi for the evening aarti.
Dhamek Stupa Sarnath Museum Ashoka Pillar Mulagandha Kuti Vihar
04
Day 4
Varanasi to Allahabad (Prayagraj) — The Sangam
Drive 120 km to Prayagraj (Allahabad). The highlight is the Triveni Sangam — the sacred confluence of the Ganga, Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati rivers. Take a boat to the confluence point and witness the spiritual power of this site. Visit Allahabad Fort (Akbar's massive riverside fort), Anand Bhavan (Nehru family home), and the serene Swaraj Bhavan.
Triveni Sangam Sangam Boat Ride Allahabad Fort Anand Bhavan
05
Day 5
Allahabad — Temples & Heritage Sites
Morning visit to the Mankameshwar Temple and Alopi Devi Mandir. Explore the Khusro Bagh — a Mughal-era walled garden with ornate tombs. Visit the Allahabad Museum for its unique collection of ancient sculptures and Nehru memorabilia. Evening at leisure by the Yamuna riverfront.
Mankameshwar Temple Alopi Devi Mandir Khusro Bagh Allahabad Museum
06
Day 6
Allahabad to Ayodhya — Birthplace of Lord Ram
Drive 160 km to Ayodhya — one of the seven sacred cities of Hinduism and the birthplace of Lord Ram. The journey culminates at the magnificent new Ram Mandir — a monumental temple built at the exact birthplace (Ram Janmabhoomi). Visit Hanuman Garhi, Kanak Bhawan, and take an evening aarti at Ram ki Paidi on the banks of the Sarayu river.
Ram Mandir Hanuman Garhi Kanak Bhawan Sarayu Aarti
07
Day 7–8 (Optional Extension)
Ayodhya Leisure / Return Journey
Optional extra day for a deeper Ayodhya exploration — Birla Temple, Tulsi Smarak Bhawan, and Valmiki Bhawan. Or a day trip to Chitrakoot (the forest where Ram, Sita, and Lakshman spent their exile). Return journey to Varanasi or Lucknow airport for onward flights, or drive directly to Delhi (approx. 7 hrs via Lucknow Expressway).
Birla Temple Chitrakoot (Optional) Tulsi Smarak Bhawan Departure / Airport Drop

Before You Go

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit
October to March is ideal — pleasant weather, clear festival calendars, and comfortable for outdoor ghats. Avoid May–June (intense heat). Kumbh Mela years (Prayagraj) draw millions — plan around it or join it!
Temple Dress Code
Cover shoulders and legs at all temples. Remove footwear before entering shrines — carry a cloth bag or use the temple's shoe counter. Bright, traditional Indian clothing is warmly welcomed at pilgrimage sites.
Ganga Aarti Timings
The Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat happens every evening at sunset (approximately 6:30 PM in winter, 7:15 PM in summer). Arrive 30 minutes early to get a good spot. Watching from a boat gives the most spectacular view.
Respectful Travel
Photography inside temple sanctuaries may be restricted — always ask. Avoid leather items at temple entrances. The narrow lanes (galis) of Varanasi's old city are best explored on foot — our driver will park nearby and wait.
